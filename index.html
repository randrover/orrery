Concepts (53)

Machinery & chemistry

  • splicing — the process: introns/exons, two-step transesterification chemistry
  • spliceosome — the machine: five snRNPs, assembly cycle, RNA-based catalysis (ribozyme)
  • splice-sites — 5’ss, branch point, polypyrimidine tract, 3’ss; recognition & degenerate consensus
  • branch-site — the intronic branchpoint adenosine recognized by U2 snRNA during spliceosome assembly
  • cryptic-splice-sites — non-canonical splice sites resembling consensus, usually deleterious, actively suppressed
  • exon-junction-complex — three-protein core (EIF4A3/RBM8A/MAGOH) deposited upstream of exon–exon junctions by the spliceosome
  • ejc-modularity — a common EJC core acquiring alternative peripheral modules (RNPS1-EJC vs CASC3-EJC)
  • ejc-target-specificity — why only ~60–80% of exon–exon junctions carry an EJC
  • recursive-splicing — multi-step removal of long introns via re-splicing sites at exon–exon junctions
  • snrna — the core small nuclear RNAs (U1, U2, U4, U5, U6) forming the catalytic heart of the spliceosome

Regulation

  • splicing-regulatory-elements — exonic/intronic enhancers & silencers (ESE/ESS/ISE/ISS)
  • splicing-factors — SR proteins (activators) vs hnRNPs (repressors); tissue-specific factors
  • rna-binding-proteins — RBPs in splicing; motif reading; disease-linked prion-like domains
  • splicing-code — predicting isoforms from sequence; PWMs → deep learning (SpliceAI, Pangolin)
  • rna-structure-and-splicing — structural layer: RNA–RNA base pairing & G-quadruplexes mask/expose splice sites
  • akt-splicing-regulation — AKT as a CASC3-EJC constituent that phosphorylates SR proteins / SR-protein kinases
  • intronic-splicing-enhancer — cis-regulatory intron element promoting splicing of a nearby exon (ISE)
  • m6a-methylation — N6-methyladenosine mRNA modification (METTL3–METTL14) near junctions, modulating splicing
  • metabolic-shielding — cationic polyamines binding acidic phosphorylatable motifs to block SR/SF phosphorylation
  • microexons — very short (3–27 nt) alternatively spliced exons with weak splice sites, enriched in the nervous system
  • rna-splicing-map — positional framework: an RBP’s binding location relative to a splice site sets its activator/repressor role
  • transposable-element-exonization — co-option of transposon/ERV sequence into spliced exons, creating new exonic sequence

Timing & coupling

  • cotranscriptional-splicing — splicing during transcription; coupling to Pol II elongation
  • post-transcriptional-splicing — splicing after transcription; intron retention; detained introns
  • splicing-and-chromatin — histone marks, adapters, Pol II speed, chromatin retention
  • detained-intron — an internal intron spliced more slowly than its neighbors, accumulating in nuclear transcripts

Outcomes & significance

  • alternative-splicing — event types, prevalence, proteome expansion, functionality debate
  • intron-retention — a widespread regulatory AS event type; detained introns, AS–NMD
  • circular-rna — covalently closed RNAs from back-splicing; detection challenges
  • biomolecular-condensates — LLPS/membraneless platforms (nuclear speckles) regulating splicing
  • splicing-and-disease — splice-site & splicing-factor mutations in disease and cancer
  • splicing-therapeutics — ASOs, small molecules, the first approved splicing drugs
  • splicing-and-evolution — AS scaling with complexity; drift vs selection; adaptations
  • alternative-polyadenylation — 3′-end cleavage / poly(A)-site selection generating mRNA isoforms with different 3′UTRs
  • nonsense-mediated-decay — cytoplasmic surveillance degrading PTC-bearing transcripts; couples to AS as a regulatory switch
  • unproductive-splicing — AS outcomes (poison exons, retained introns, cryptic junctions) yielding PTC-containing, NMD-targeted mRNA
  • nuclear-speckles — membraneless nuclear bodies enriched in splicing factors that raise local spliceosome concentration
  • antisense-oligonucleotides — short synthetic oligos that base-pair pre-mRNA to redirect splice-site / exon choice (ASOs)
  • oligonucleotide-therapeutics — RNA-targeting modalities redirecting splicing/polyadenylation (ASOs, RNase-H, siRNA)

Methods

  • splicing-methods — toolkit for studying AS: long-read, single-cell/spatial, CRISPR, sQTL, deep learning
  • percent-spliced-in — ψ / PSI and ΔPSI: the standard event-based metric for (differential) alternative splicing
