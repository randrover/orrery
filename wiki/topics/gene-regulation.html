Gene Regulation & cis-Regulatory Genomics

Topic hub for how the noncoding genome controls gene expression — the enhancers, promoters, the [[cis-regulatory-grammar|sequence grammar]] that transcription factors read, the enhancer–promoter logic that connects them, and the reference maps and models that let us interpret regulatory variation. This is the regulatory-biology counterpart to the wiki’s sequence-model work (topic artificial-intelligence), its 3d-genome architecture, and the disease genetics that regulatory variants feed (brain-development-neurogenomics, splicing).

Why it matters

Most trait- and disease-associated variation is noncoding and regulatory (noncoding-variants) — it changes when, where, and how much a gene is expressed rather than the protein. Reading it requires (a) a catalogue of the regulatory elements (cis-regulatory-elements), (b) the grammar of how sequence encodes activity (cis-regulatory-grammar), and (c) the wiring between elements and their target genes (chromatin-loops). This topic assembles the experimental and reference foundations; the predictive models that learn this grammar live under artificial-intelligence.

Current synthesis

  • From cataloguing to grammar to wiring. The topic runs from finding enhancers (p300 ChIP-seq, eRNA, ENCODE cCREs) to reading their sequence grammar (cis-regulatory-grammar) to connecting elements to genes — where the emerging picture is broad enhancer–promoter compatibility (bergman-2022-compatibility-rules-of-human-enhancer) that becomes more instructive and physically closer as cells differentiate (pollex-2024-enhancer-promoter-interactions-become-more, chen-2024-increased-enhancer-promoter-interactions-during).
  • Regulatory variation is coordinated and mostly noncoding. Sequence variants perturb TF binding, chromatin, and transcription in a haplotype-coordinated way with TFs as primary drivers (kilpinen-2013-coordinated-effects-of-sequence-variation); interpreting such noncoding-variants is the throughline into disease genetics.
  • Regulation evolves and is layered. cis-regulatory divergence (often transposable-element-driven) underlies neocortex evolution (zemke-2023-conserved-and-divergent-gene-regulatory), and an epitranscriptomic (m6A) layer tunes enhancer/TE activity and chromatin accessibility (sun-2023-crosstalk-between-rna-m6a, li-2023-super-enhancer-rna-m6a-promotes-local).
