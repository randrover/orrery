Brain Development & Neuro-genomics

Topic hub for how the human brain is built and how its genetics goes wrong — neurodevelopment (neurogenesis → cell types → circuits), human-brain-evolution, and psychiatric-genetics. This is the biological + disease context for the wiki’s brain-RNA work: the developing-brain sQTL/isoform atlases (under splicing) and the single-cell/MPRA methods (under functional-single-cell-genomics) live next door; this topic supplies the cells, stages, and disorders they are mapping.

Why it matters

Most neuropsychiatric risk is polygenic and acts early in development (grotzinger-2025-mapping-the-genetic-landscape-across). Reading that risk requires knowing the developmental program (neurodevelopment), the cell-type “parts list” (neuronal-cell-types), and human-specific biology (human-brain-evolution) — and an experimental model to test causality (brain-organoids).

Current synthesis (across the 26 sources)

  • Build, then break. The topic runs from how the brain is built — neurogenesis (proneural specification, adult niches), migration/lamination, and the diversification into neuronal-cell-types — to how its genetics goes wrong. A repeated lesson: transcriptomic identity alone under-specifies cells (connectivity matters, kim-2020-extraction-of-distinct-neuronal-cell), which is exactly the resolution bulk genomics lacks.
  • Human-specific and slow. Human brain distinctiveness is framed as expanded progenitors + prolonged (“neotenic”) development + HAR/duplication innovations, best dissected in brain-organoids (zhou-2023-genetics-of-human-brain, lindhout-2024-a-molecular-and-cellular-perspective, kelley-2021-human-brain-organogenesis-toward-cellular).
  • Disease is developmental, polygenic, and convergent. Cross-disorder risk is shared across 14 conditions, enriched in early development and specific cells (grotzinger-2025-mapping-the-genetic-landscape-across); ASD shows broad cortical transcriptomic convergence (gandal-2022-broad-transcriptomic-dysregulation-occurs-across) and developmental heterogeneity (zhang-2025-polygenic-and-developmental-profiles-of); normative developmental expression axes link to ASD/SCZ across imaging + genetics (dear-2024-cortical-gene-expression-architecture-links).
  • Convergence runs deeper than transcription — and earlier than birth. Across 11 genetically diverse mouse models, ASD mutations converge on a transient radial-glial developmental delay (largest signal in chromatin-accessibility, early-postnatal synaptic/ion-channel downregulation, larger female effects) that fades with maturation (schwarz-2026-cortical-development-dynamics-across-autism); meanwhile the human ASD cortical methylome (dna-methylation) shows only modest, cell-type-specific changes that are uncorrelated with expression and dwarfed by aging (eyring-2026-single-cell-profiling-of-dna-methylation) — pushing the field toward cell-type- and stage-resolved regulatory readouts beyond bulk transcriptomics.
  • Causal mapping: when and which cell type. Beyond correlating risk with development, conditional-genetics papers now pin ASD mechanisms to a specific window and cell population — chd8 haploinsufficiency acts in midfetal (~E14.5) ventral/ganglionic-eminence progenitors, and restoring CHD8 there rescues behavior (nitahara-2026-defective-ventral-neurogenesis-midfetal-chd8); timed reactivation logic parallels SCN2A (tamura-2025-crispr-activation-for-scn2a-related, scn2a-conditional-ki-mouse) and the assembloid interneuron screen (meng-2023-assembloid-crispr-screens-reveal-impact) — CRISPR-based tools (topic crispr) are what make these causal tests possible.
  • The bridge to RNA work. This topic is the why behind the brain-RNA atlases: it explains the cells and stages that developing-brain splicing-qtl/isoform studies (walker-2019-genetic-control-of-expression-and, wen-2024-cross-ancestry-atlas-of-gene-isoform) and single-cell methods (functional-single-cell-genomics) are mapping — and why cell-type and developmental resolution are the field’s open frontiers.

Open questions

  • Which developmental window and cell type does each disorder’s risk act in, causally? (grotzinger-2025-mapping-the-genetic-landscape-across, psychiatric-genetics)
  • Can organoids faithfully model the human-specific and late features where disease risk concentrates? (brain-organoids, human-brain-evolution)
  • How to annotate cell types when transcriptome ≠ identity (connectivity, projection)? (kim-2020-extraction-of-distinct-neuronal-cell, neuronal-cell-types)

Cross-topic: the brain RNA/isoform genetics (splicing) and single-cell/MPRA resources (functional-single-cell-genomics) of developing human cortex are the molecular instruments mapping the biology framed here.
