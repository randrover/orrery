Splicing

Topic hub for pre-mRNA splicing and alternative splicing — the removal of introns from precursor mRNA by the spliceosome and its regulation, which together form a central layer of eukaryotic gene regulation. The wiki’s deepest topic: this page curates the sources (grouped below), the concept map, and the current synthesis.

Why it matters

Splicing converts intron-containing “mosaic” genes into mature mRNA via a two-step transesterification reaction. alternative-splicing — the regulated inclusion/exclusion of exons — lets one gene produce many isoforms, expanding the proteome and underpinning organismal complexity, development, disease, and therapy (see editorial-2025-rna-splicing-a-central-layer).

Current synthesis (across the 80 sources)

  • The brain is splicing’s deep end. A large cluster maps [[splicing-and-neurodevelopment|splicing across brain development, aging and disease]]: neuron-specific regulators (PTBP/Rbfox/Nova/SRRM4) drive timed exon programs (su-2018-alternative-splicing-in-neurogenesis-and, weyn-vanhentenryck-2018-precise-temporal-regulation-of-alternative); neurons diverge from the splicing-code more than glia (michielsen-2026-decoding-exon-inclusion-in); cross-ancestry developmental e/sQTL atlases show isoform/splicing regulation mediates the most neuropsychiatric GWAS heritability (wen-2024-cross-ancestry-atlas-of-gene-isoform, walker-2019-genetic-control-of-expression-and, li-2018-integrative-functional-genomic-analysis); and disease links span autism (parikshak-2016-genome-wide-changes-in-lncrna), Huntington’s (nguyen-2025-aberrant-splicing-in-huntingtons-disease) and druggable poison-exons (pigini-2026-poison-exon-splicing-in, neil-2025-poison-exons-tuning-rna-splicing). rna-editing is tracked as a sibling RNA-processing layer.
  • Long reads are resolving isoforms directly. A 2024–2026 wave of long-read-rna-seq moves splicing from event-inference to full-length isoform observation — single-cell/spatial (longcell), cis/trans separation by phasing (isolaser, allele-specific-splicing), cell-type isoform atlases powering sQTL discovery (isomiga, aida), direct RNA-seq reading modifications and splicing together (molm13-drna-seq), and exposing ancestry bias in reference annotation (poder-annotation, a data-quality-and-bias instance). Clinically, splicing programs define and treat disease subtypes (oncosplice, leukemia).
  • The machine is settled; the regulation is the frontier. The spliceosome is a ~4.8 MDa dynamic RNP of five snRNPs whose catalytic core is RNA — a ribozyme related to group II introns (sharp-1994-split-genes-and-rna-splicing, lee-2015-mechanisms-and-regulation-of-alternative); cryo-EM resolved most states (rogalska-2022-regulation-of-pre-mrna-splicing). Open questions are about control, not chemistry.
  • AS is nearly universal. Founding mRNA-seq surveys put AS at ~92–95% of multiexon human genes (wang-2008-alternative-isoform-regulation-in-human, pan-2008-deep-surveying-of-alternative-splicing); it expands the proteome (nilsen-2010-expansion-of-the-eukaryotic-proteome) and is read out per-event as PSI.
  • A position-dependent, combinatorial code. Weak metazoan splice-sites + splicing-regulatory-elements read by splicing-factors/rna-binding-proteins in a context-dependent way (fu-2014-context-dependent-control-of-alternative-splicing, witten-2011-understanding-splicing-regulation-through-rna, wang-2008-splicing-regulation-from-a-parts); RBP concentration itself gates outcomes (begg-2020-concentration-dependent-splicing-is-enabled). Modeling this splicing-code went from PWMs to deep learning (SpliceAI, darts, capitanchik-2024-from-computational-models-of-the).
  • A mature measurement ecosystem. Splicing is quantified two ways — isoform-based (cufflinks, kallisto, suppa) and event-based (rmats/rmats-turbo, miso, dexseq, leafcutter, majiq-v2, psi-sigma, splicetrap) — over a decade of rna-seq tooling (sousa-luis-2026-tools-and-tactics-for-studying, conesa-2016-survey-of-best-practices, stark-2019-rna-sequencing-the-teenage-years). Tools disagree, so orthogonal validation matters.
  • Genetics connects sequence to splicing to disease. Population RNA-seq maps sQTLs and allele-specific splicing (park-2018-the-expanding-landscape-of-alternative, zhang-2020-regional-variation-of-splicing-qtls, demirdjian-2020-detecting-allele-specific-alternative-splicing); RBP binding/function atlases (van-nostrand-2020-a-large-scale-binding-and-functional) and isoform/proteome catalogues (mironov-2021-an-extended-catalogue-of-tandem, lau-2019-splice-junction-based-mapping-of) ground the regulatory logic.
  • Timing is itself regulatory. Splicing is largely co-transcriptional, but a large fraction is post-transcriptional (choquet-2025-the-regulation-and-function-of, shine-2024-co-transcriptional-gene-regulation-in), and intron-retention is a widespread regulatory event.
  • Mechanism → medicine. Splicing-factor mutations drive cancer and genetic disease; the first splicing therapies are approved (nusinersen, risdiplam, eteplirsen) — see scotti-2015-rna-mis-splicing-in-disease, splicing-therapeutics, splicing-and-disease.
