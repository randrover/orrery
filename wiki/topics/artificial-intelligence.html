Artificial Intelligence

Topic hub for AI/ML in biology and genomics — both the methods (foundation models and deep learning for sequences, structures, single cells, and perturbations) and the safety, dual-use, and governance questions they raise. The wiki’s second topic (alongside splicing and functional-single-cell-genomics).

Why it matters

Since the transformer (2017), foundation-models for biological sequences (DNA, RNA, protein) and single cells have become central tools in genomics. They now span structure prediction, regulatory-grammar modelling, mutation-effect prediction, and perturbation response — while the same generative capability that designs beneficial molecules can lower the barrier to harmful ones, keeping innovation vs safety in tension (hassan-2026-a-safety-centric-perspective-on-innovation).

Current synthesis (across the 7 sources)

  • Foundation models are now general-purpose biological tools. The same paradigm spans DNA language models (crossdna), protein language models (uniair), RNA structure (trrosettarna2), and single-cell embeddings (the FMs signature-tool interprets) — a clear arc from the splicing-code/spliceai origins into every biomolecular modality.
  • Inject domain priors, win efficiency. The strongest methods bake biology into the architecture rather than scaling blindly: secondary-structure priors (trrosettarna2), explicit cross-strand symmetry (crossdna, 408K params beating 7B Evo2), gene knowledge-graphs (txpert), and optimal-transport structure (cmonge). Several beat models orders of magnitude larger.
  • Generalization to the unseen is the shared frontier. Unseen drugs/doses (cmonge), unseen perturbations and cell lines (txpert), unseen mutations (uniair), and unseen RNA conformers (trrosettarna2) — all foreground OOD generalization, and all hit limits tied to data-quality-and-bias.
  • Interpretation and measurement check the models. signature-tool turns FM embeddings into testable gene-importance hypotheses (model-interpretability); and across the wiki, direct measurement (massively-parallel-reporter-assay, ΔPSI) still beats prediction where models are weak.
  • Safety stays the backdrop. Capability gains (immunogen/antibody design in uniair, sequence generation) keep dual-use-risk live; no single safeguard suffices, motivating layered ai-safety + ai-governance (hassan-2026-a-safety-centric-perspective-on-innovation).

Open questions

  • How to govern foundation-model development that largely does not yet meet “high-risk” standards? (ai-governance)
  • How to make safeguards robust to novel sequences, not just known threats? (ai-safety)
  • Can sequence models predict individual-level and activating effects, where they currently fail? (yang-2026-explicit-dynamic-cross-strand-interactions, groover-2026-mapping-cis-regulatory-mutations-at)
  • Will single-cell perturbation models generalize across cell types and beat simple baselines? (driessen-2026-conditional-monge-gap-enables-generalizable, wenkel-2026-txpert-using-multiple-knowledge)
