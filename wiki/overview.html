Current thesis — topic: splicing

RNA splicing is a central layer of gene regulation, and across these sources the field’s center of gravity has shifted from how splicing works to how it is controlled and read:

  1. The machinery is essentially solved. The spliceosome is a large, dynamic, RNA-catalysed machine (a ribozyme related to group II introns), and cryo-EM has resolved most of its states (lee-2015-mechanisms-and-regulation-of-alternative, rogalska-2022-regulation-of-pre-mrna-splicing). Remaining questions are about regulation, not chemistry.
  2. Outcomes are set by a position-dependent code. Weak metazoan splice-sites + splicing-regulatory-elements read by splicing-factors determine isoform choice. Modeling this splicing-code has progressed from position weight matrices to deep-learning models that now interpret ~10% of pathogenic rare-disease variants (capitanchik-2024-from-computational-models-of-the).
  3. Timing is itself regulatory. Splicing is largely co-transcriptional, but a large fraction (~40%) is post-transcriptional-splicing, gating mRNA export and translation (choquet-2025-the-regulation-and-function-of).
  4. Function vs noise is unresolved. alternative-splicing occurs in ~95–100% of human multiexon genes, but how much is functional is debated (see Open questions).
  5. Mechanism is becoming medicine. Splicing-factor mutations drive cancer and genetic disease (splicing-and-disease); the first splicing-targeted drugs are approved (splicing-therapeutics).
  6. A mature measurement-and-genetics stack underpins all of it. A decade-plus of rna-seq tooling now quantifies splicing two ways — isoform-based (cufflinks, kallisto, suppa) and event-based (rmats/rmats-turbo, miso, leafcutter, majiq-v2, dexseq) — and population genetics maps sQTLs linking variation to disease (park-2018-the-expanding-landscape-of-alternative). Tools disagree, so the open methodological question is which readout to trust when.

Open questions

  • Functionality of AS — ✅ Mostly reconciled (see alternative-splicing-functionality-debate): the apparent marasco-2022-the-physiology-of-alternative (functional) vs wright-2022-alternative-splicing-as-a-source (noise) clash is largely a difference in the definition of “functional”, the denominator counted, and the evidence weighted — not a factual contradiction. Both agree on a conserved functional core; residual uncertainty is the size of the low-abundance tail, resolvable only with long-read/single-cell/CRISPR data.
  • Co- vs post-transcriptional timing trigger — mechanism unknown (choquet-2025-the-regulation-and-function-of).
  • Interpretable, tissue-specific splicing-code models — still open (capitanchik-2024-from-computational-models-of-the).

Current thesis — topic: artificial-intelligence

Across ten sources, foundation-models have become general-purpose biological tools spanning every modality: DNA (crossdna), protein (uniair), RNA structure (trrosettarna2), single cells (the FMs signature-tool interprets), and single-cell dynamics (regvelo couples RNA velocity with a learnable gene regulatory network). Two patterns recur: (1) injecting domain priors beats blind scale — secondary-structure priors, explicit cross-strand symmetry, gene knowledge-graphs, and optimal-transport let small models beat ones orders of magnitude larger; (2) generalization to the unseen (drugs, cell lines, mutations, conformers) is the shared frontier, limited by data-quality-and-bias, and often checked by direct measurement (massively-parallel-reporter-assay, ΔPSI) where models are weak. Safety remains the backdrop: generative capability keeps dual-use-risk immediate and no single ai-safety safeguard suffices, motivating layered measures plus ai-governance (hassan-2026-a-safety-centric-perspective-on-innovation). The newest entry pushes deep learning into population genetics: flare scores billions of noncoding-variant effects from chromatin-accessibility models and folds in evolutionary constraint, linking the AI methods to functional-single-cell-genomics atlases and the biometrical-genetics missing-heritability debate (marderstein-2026-decoding-common-and-rare-noncoding). A second statistical-genetics entry pushes expression imputation beyond cis: co-expression-network models (ingene-module) inject distal trans signal (gene-coexpression-network) into TWAS, expanding the imputable transcriptome and surfacing hundreds of new schizophrenia genes in human brain (rossi-2026-coexpression-based-models-improve-eqtl).

Current thesis — topic: 3d-genome

One source so far (che-2026-multiscale-evolution-of-the-3d-genome): nuclear genome-architecture (loops → TADs → compartments) is an evolvable trait. It evolves fastest in cancer (3d-genome-and-disease), diverges between close species mainly via cis-regulatory-elements within conserved frameworks, and recurs across kingdoms through convergent evolution with distinct machinery (3d-genome-evolution). Thin until more sources are ingested.

Current thesis — topic: functional-single-cell-genomics

These sources frame the measurement / resource counterpart to the AI methods: a single-cell proteomic atlas of the developing human brain (wu-2026-single-cell-proteomic-landscape-of, single-cell-proteomics), an MPRA mapping >30,000 cis-regulatory mutations in sorghum (groover-2026-mapping-cis-regulatory-mutations-at, massively-parallel-reporter-assay), and digital twins of ex vivo human lungs (zhou-2026-digital-twins-of-ex, digital-twins). The unifying thesis: direct, high-throughput measurement still beats prediction where models are weakest (mRNA→protein inference, activating regulatory variants), and these resources are exactly the atlases, variant maps, and platforms the artificial-intelligence topic’s models learn from and are tested against. The endogenous-perturbation foundations that grew up here — the pooled-crispr-screen family — have now been promoted to their own crispr topic. What remains in this topic is the reporter-assay / atlas measurement stack, including a human-neural massively-parallel-reporter-assay of eight psychiatric disorders (lee-2024-massively-parallel-reporter-assay-psychiatric) that turns regulatory correlation into causation and validates the variants the artificial-intelligence models nominate.

Current thesis — topic: crispr

Twelve sources trace CRISPR-Cas across a single arc — mechanism → tool → screen → therapy → safety. It began as the only known prokaryotic adaptive immune system (hille-2016-crispr-cas-biology-mechanisms-and-relevance, mohanraju-2016-diverse-evolutionary-roots-and-mechanistic), was repurposed into a democratized genome-editing toolkit (barrangou-2016-applications-of-crispr-technologies-in, wang-2023-crispr-technology-a-decade-of, pacesa-2024-past-present-and-future-of), and scaled into the pooled-crispr-screen family — genome-wide screens (wang-2014-genetic-screens-in-human-cells), transcriptome-readout single-cell screens (perturb-seq), tiling deletions (scandel), and precise-variant editing (crispey). Its non-cutting branch (crispri-crispra) drives dosage screens and therapy. The frontier is the clinic: in vivo neuronal editing (park-2019-in-vivo-neuronal-gene-editing) and CRISPRa dosage rescue (tamura-2025-crispr-activation-for-scn2a-related) push toward treatment, where measuring and bounding off-target risk becomes decisive (stewart-2025-measurement-and-clinical-interpretation-of). Deep dive: crispr-screens-and-genome-editing.

Current thesis — topic: brain-development-neurogenomics

Twenty-five sources frame the biology + disease context for the brain-RNA work: how the human brain is built (neurodevelopment, neurogenesis, neuronal-cell-types, brain-organoids), what makes it human (human-brain-evolution), and how its genetics goes wrong — neuropsychiatric risk is polygenic, pleiotropic, and acts early in development, converging on specific cells and heavily on autism (grotzinger-2025-mapping-the-genetic-landscape-across, gandal-2022-broad-transcriptomic-dysregulation-occurs-across, psychiatric-genetics). A recurring lesson — transcriptome ≠ cell identity (kim-2020-extraction-of-distinct-neuronal-cell) — is exactly the resolution bulk genomics lacks, so this topic explains which cells and stages the developing-brain splicing-qtl/isoform atlases and single-cell methods are mapping, and why cell-type + developmental resolution is the open frontier. The newest ASD pair sharpens this: 11 mouse models converge on a transient radial-glial delay that fades with maturation (schwarz-2026-cortical-development-dynamics-across-autism), while the human ASD cortical methylome shows only modest cell-type-specific change, uncorrelated with expression and dwarfed by aging (eyring-2026-single-cell-profiling-of-dna-methylation). The newest entry adds the missing context itself: population-scale neuronal-activation (KCl-stimulated iPSC Glut/GABA neurons, 100 donors) shows most NPD genetic effects are activation-specific — stimulation-driven eQTLs, caQTLs and ASoC (caQTLs explaining ~2.5× more SCZ heritability than eQTLs) feed ctwas to prioritize risk genes the resting brain misses, and implicate an activation-regulated cholesterol axis (liang-2026-single-cell-multiomics-of-neuron). CRISPR now enters this topic as a tool: pooled screens in forebrain assembloids and differentiating neurons map disease genes onto interneuron-migration and differentiation (meng-2023-assembloid-crispr-screens-reveal-impact, amelan-2026-crispr-knockout-screens-reveal-genes), while CRISPRa dosage rescue and in vivo editing move toward therapy for neurodegeneration and NDDs (tamura-2025-crispr-activation-for-scn2a-related, park-2019-in-vivo-neuronal-gene-editing) — see the crispr topic. The experimental toolkit for dissecting NDD complexity is reviewed in sun-2024-strategies-for-dissecting-the-complexity.
