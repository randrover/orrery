Tools and tactics for studying alternative splicing

One-line: A methods/tools review of the modern toolkit for studying alternative-splicing — short- and long-read sequencing (bulk, single-cell, spatial), CRISPR-based functional assays, minigene/reporter assays, population/sQTL genetics, and deep-learning splicing-code models — organized around the goals of building a complete mRNA-isoform atlas, testing isoform function, and decoding the genetic control of splicing. Complements the wiki’s biology-focused sources by cataloguing how splicing is actually measured and perturbed.

Key takeaways

  • splicing-methods for alternative-splicing have evolved through a timeline (Fig. 2): splicing/AS discovery (1977) → RT-PCR (1989) → ESTs (1991) → exon-junction microarrays (2003) → high-throughput RNA-seq (2008) → long-read full-length sequencing (2013) and single-cell isoform RNA-seq → spatially resolved full-length mRNA sequencing (2023). Two landmark RNA-seq studies estimated 92-95% of human multi-exon genes undergo AS.
  • AS forms catalogued: cassette-exon inclusion/skipping, alternative 5’/3’ splice-sites (exon elongation/truncation), intron retention, plus alternative TSS and polyadenylation; these are frequently coordinated, and long-read data show coordinated/independent splicing of distant exons and that intron-removal order need not follow transcription order (cotranscriptional-splicing).
  • Short-read RNA-seq analysis splits into isoform-based vs count-based (exon-based and event-based) methods; event-based tools compute per cent spliced-in (PSI) and compare ΔPSI. Splice-aware aligners (STAR, HISAT2), pseudo-aligners (Salmon, Kallisto) and de novo assemblers (Trinity, Oases, SPLASH) are standard. Benchmarks show substantial tool-to-tool discrepancy → orthogonal validation needed.
  • Long-read platforms (PacBio SMRT/HiFi, ONT) directly capture full-length isoforms and exon connectivity, resolving complex patterns short reads cannot; ONT direct RNA-seq additionally reads native RNA + modifications (m6A) without RT/PCR.
  • CRISPR-based functional assays directly test isoform function: Cas9 splice-site/exon editing and pooled fitness screens, base editors for precise splice-site/enhancer disruption, and Cas13/dCasRx for RNA-level, isoform-specific, reversible perturbation and SRE mapping (splicing-regulatory-elements, splicing-factors).
  • Genetic control is mapped via sQTL analysis, allele-specific expression (ASE), and massively parallel reporter assays (MPRAs); a recent GTEx v8 analysis found a substantial fraction of trait/disease variants act through splicing.
  • Deep-learning splicing-code models progressed from feature-based predictors to CNNs (spliceai, Pangolin) and newer multimodal/transformer/foundation models (DeltaSplice, Borzoi, DNABERT, SpliceBERT, AlphaGenome); none yet capture full cell-type/condition-specific isoform repertoires.
  • Central open debate: distinguishing functional isoforms from pervasive “splicing noise” — many AS isoforms are low-abundance and may be nonfunctional mis-splicing, but a subset is biologically meaningful.

Notable claims / data

  • Platforms (Table 2): Illumina >99.9% base accuracy, reads 50-300 bp; PacBio HiFi ~99.8% accuracy, 10-25 kb reads; ONT cDNA reads 10-20 kb avg (>1 Mb possible), ONT direct RNA ~1-1.3 kb avg (some >20 kb). PacBio/ONT continuous reads average 10-20 kb.
  • Library prep matters: rRNA is ~80% of total RNA; polyA selection generally preferred over rRNA depletion for splicing; UMIs remove PCR duplicates; strand-specific and paired-end improve junction calling. Typical AS depth 30-60M reads/sample; 100-200M for low-abundance isoforms or poorly annotated genomes.
  • Short-read limit: <300 bp reads vs ~3 kb average human mRNA → poor full-length reconstruction.
  • Large isoform-atlas efforts: ONT RNA-seq of GTEx samples found >70,000 novel transcripts; PacBio added to ENCODE4 to catalogue isoforms across 81 human/mouse samples (preprint). Ouro-Seq built a mouse single-cell long-read splicing atlas of >100,000 cells across 12 organs.
  • Single-cell: droplet 3’/5’ methods (10x Chromium) capture only transcript ends — insufficient for junctions; full-length plate methods (Smart-seq2/3, RamDA-seq, VASA-seq, FLASH-seq) enable event-level AS in single cells. Single-cell long-read methods: ScISOr-Seq2, scTaILoR-seq (>1,000 genes), HIT-scISOseq, MAS-ISO-seq, Ouro-Seq, snISOr-Seq. m6Anet quantifies m6A from ONT direct RNA.
  • Targeted long-read: nanopore amplicon sequencing resolved ~8,000 full-length cDNAs from four Drosophila genes incl. Dscam1 (most alternatively spliced gene known); RNA CaptureSeq enriches selected transcripts.
  • Spatial: MERFISH/seqFISH (imaging), barcoding methods (original 2016 spatial transcriptomics, Slide-seq, Seq-Scope, Stereo-seq, Stereo-cell); Spl-ISO-Seq did spatial long-read isoforms at 10-µm resolution (human cortex > white matter splicing regulation); Longcell pipeline handles single-cell/spatial Nanopore barcode/UMI errors.
  • Proteomics: most shotgun workflows use trypsin and miss splice-unique peptides; a study using six proteases + deep LC-MS found most frame-preserving AS isoforms are translated. Ribosome profiling and polyribosome long-read RNA-seq probe which isoforms are translated; nanopore protein sequencing emerging for proteoforms.
  • Functional-prediction tools: NEASE (pathway/PPI interpretation of AS), FAScore (ML predictor of functional exon-skipping), ORF cloning for high-throughput isoform interaction profiling (alternative isoforms share <half their binding partners). AlphaFold3 enables AS structural impact studies.
  • CRISPR examples: sgRNAs at MPRIP exon 23 splice sites → skipping → metastasis; targeting an RBFOX2 motif → inclusion → metastasis suppression (pancreatic cancer). Base-editor screen showed TAF5 exon 8 AS affects transcription. SpliceRUSH = high-throughput dCas13 SRE mapping; dCasRx fused to splicing factors (e.g. dCasRx-RBM25) activates exon inclusion at scale.
  • Reporter assays: splicing reporters tested >2 million random sequences for splice-site selection (predicts variants in SMN1, SMN2, CFTR, BRCA2); an MPRA of ~5,000 exonic disease mutations found ~10% disrupt splicing vs only 3% of common SNPs. EXSISERS quantifies endogenous protein isoforms (e.g. MAPT exon 10) via intein splicing in patient iPS cells.
  • sQTL/ASE tools: scNanoGPS (genotype + splicing from single-cell nanopore), LORALS (allele-specific long-read analysis). siRNA knockdown of 305 spliceosome/regulatory proteins reconstructed splicing-factor functional networks.
  • Splicing-code benchmark: spliceai (CNN, splice-site positions) and Pangolin (quantitative, multi-tissue) achieve highest accuracy among variant predictors, but performance is limited especially for exonic variants.

Connections

  • Methods companion to the wiki’s mechanism/code sources. Directly complements capitanchik-2024-from-computational-models-of-the on the splicing-code and spliceai/Pangolin (both flag exonic-variant and tissue-specific prediction as the weak spots; both name DeltaSplice, Borzoi, SpliceBERT, foundation models). This paper situates those models as the “decode” layer atop the sequencing/perturbation toolkit.
  • Provides the experimental backbone for the AS functionality debate: long-read isoform atlases reveal vast diversity, while CRISPR screens, ORF-interaction assays, proteomics (six-protease MS, ribosome profiling) and FAScore are the tactics for separating functional isoforms from mis-splicing noise (its closing “Do rare isoforms matter?” question).
  • post-transcriptional-splicing / cotranscriptional-splicing: long-read data show intron-removal order ≠ transcription order and coordinated distant-exon splicing; subcellular-fractionation long-read RNA-seq links isoforms to translational fate; ONT direct RNA couples isoform structure to m6A epitranscriptome.
  • splicing-and-disease: minigene/MPRA validation, sQTL/ASE genetics, CRISPR isogenic iPS-cell models (photoreceptors, cardiomyocytes); examples in NRXN1, NDUFB10, MAPT, and neurodevelopmental/neuropsychiatric risk via long-read neocortex isoform mapping.
  • splicing-factors / rna-binding-proteins / splicing-regulatory-elements: SpliceRUSH and dCasRx-activator screens, the 305-factor siRNA network study, and RBFOX2 editing are the tools for dissecting regulator logic.

Open questions

  • Long-read platforms still need higher accuracy/throughput for complete cell-type-specific isoform annotation from low-input RNA; low-abundance transcripts remain low-precision and require validation.
  • Need integrated computational frameworks jointly modelling transcription initiation, splicing and 3’-end formation, and spatial methods at true isoform-level single-cell/subcellular resolution.
  • Splicing is highly dynamic, but time-resolved sequencing and methods to identify transient/partial splicing states are lacking.
  • Prediction goal unmet: a model that outputs the complete isoform repertoire and relative abundances per gene in any cell type/signalling context (likely needs foundation models integrating chromatin, RBP occupancy, RNA structure, epitranscriptome).
  • Moving from descriptive cataloguing to interpretable, mechanistic models of splicing control via large-scale perturbation + RNA-protein/structure/nuclear-organization mapping.
  • The core unresolved question: how to distinguish functional isoforms from pervasive splicing noise.
