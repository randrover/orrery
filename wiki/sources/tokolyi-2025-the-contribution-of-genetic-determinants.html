The contribution of genetic determinants of blood gene expression and splicing to molecular phenotypes and health outcomes

One-line: A population-scale (n = 4,732) blood RNA-seq study from the INTERVAL cohort that maps cis/trans eQTLs and splicing QTLs (sQTLs), then integrates them with plasma protein, metabolite and lipid QTLs and disease GWAS via colocalization and mediation — repeatedly showing that splicing (especially excision of transmembrane-domain exons) is an eQTL-independent mechanism linking genetic variation to circulating protein levels and disease risk; delivered as the open-access intervalrna-portal.

Key takeaways

  • Mapped both expression and splicing QTLs in the same 4,732 blood donors: cis-eQTLs for 17,233 genes (89.9% of tested) and cis-sQTLs for 29,514 splicing events in 6,853 genes (cis-sGenes), establishing a large multi-omic splicing-qtl resource (see rna-seq).
  • Splicing events were quantified de novo / annotation-free from intron-usage ratios using the leafcutter pipeline (on Regtools-extracted junctions), not from a fixed transcript annotation — “splicing events” = differential intron usage ratios. This recovered 89.0% of GTEx whole-blood sGenes plus 4,470 new sGenes, attributed to the larger sample size and de novo excision quantification.
  • sQTLs carry information distinct from eQTLs: 82% of eQTL-colocalizing splicing events with multiple independent sQTL signals had additional sQTL loci that did NOT colocalize with the gene’s eQTL — i.e. independent regulatory layers (alternative splicing as a mechanism separate from total transcript abundance).
  • Transmembrane-domain excision is a recurring mechanism: ~98 splicing events colocalizing with pQTLs excised exons encoding transmembrane domains, generating soluble protein isoforms; many of these pQTLs did NOT colocalize with an eQTL, so the sQTL is the pivotal mechanistic clue (IL-6R, IL-7R, IL-17RA, FAS, SERPINA1, APOL1, IFNAR2, CD33).
  • Disease relevance demonstrated by multitrait colocalization + mediation across FinnGen (20 traits) and COVID-19 HGI (4 traits): WARS1 sQTL ↔ hypertension, IL7R sQTL ↔ dermatitis/eczema, OAS1 and IFNAR2 sQTLs ↔ COVID-19 severity (see splicing-and-disease).
  • The whole compendium of molecular QTLs + colocalization + mediation is released as the open-access intervalrna-portal (IntervalRNA.org.uk).

Notable claims / data

  • Discovery: 19,173 expressed autosomal genes and 111,937 de novo splicing phenotypes quantified in 11,016 genes; cis-eQTL window ±1 Mb of TSS, cis-sQTL window ±500 kb of the spliced-region center. 7,877,942 imputed variants (MAF > 0.005). FDR < 0.05.
  • Conditional/stepwise analysis: 56,959 independent cis-eQTL signals (median 3/gene, range 1–23) and 47,050 independent cis-sQTL signals (median 1 per cis-sQTL, range 1–20). Nonprimary signals existed for 81% of cis-eGenes and 49% of cis-sGenes.
  • sQTL architecture: cis-sQTL primary signals enriched in the gene body and toward the transcription end site (median 17.36 kb downstream of TSS) vs cis-eQTLs (median 5.51 kb), P = 8.42 × 10⁻²⁵⁹. Splicing events had median length 1,549 bp and excised protein-coding sequence in 32.4% of cases (rest intronic/UTR). Lead variants formed a bimodal distribution around the start/end of the sGene; median 187 bp upstream of the splicing-event center.
  • eQTL–sQTL colocalization: 3,979 of 6,252 genes had colocalized eQTL/sQTL signals; 49.0% (13,490) of tested splicing events colocalized with an eQTL for the same gene. Non-colocalizing sQTLs lay further downstream of the TSS (median 20.33 kb) than colocalizing ones (12.61 kb), P = 9.8 × 10⁻⁷⁰.
  • Trans analysis (using 53,457 conditionally independent lead cis-eSNPs): 2,058 trans-eGenes (P < 5 × 10⁻¹¹) and 644 trans-regulated splicing events / 209 trans-sGenes (P < 8.36 × 10⁻¹²). 240 splicing events (91 genes) were regulated only in trans. The RNA-binding splice factor QKI cis-eQTL trans-regulated 18 sGenes (most of any eGene); replicated GTEx trans-sQTLs for FYB1 and ABHD3. Cis-eGenes with trans-associations enriched for sequence-specific transcription factors (357/2,498, P = 1.83 × 10⁻³⁸) and C2H2 zinc-finger / KRAB domains.
  • Cross-omics colocalization (INTERVAL pQTLs/mQTLs): a cis-eQTL or cis-sQTL colocalized with cis-QTLs for 120 Olink proteins (65.9%), 404 SomaScan proteins (63.7%), 224 Nightingale metabolites (99.1%) and 495 Metabolon metabolites (81.5%); 3,430 molecular traits total. Extending to external deCODE (n = 35,559) and UKB-PPP (n = 54,219) pQTLs raised colocalizing Olink proteins from 120→1,203 and SomaScan from 404→984; replicated 95.1%/79.3% of eQTL/sQTL colocalizations in UKB-PPP and 87.0%/80.3% in deCODE.
  • Mediation: 143 cis-eGenes mediated 413 cis-eSNPs onto 202 molecular phenotypes (525 eQTL mediation triplets); 106 splicing events in 47 sGenes mediated 152 cis-sSNPs onto 50 molecular phenotypes (241 sQTL models). Net 222 molecular phenotypes significantly mediated by expression or splicing.
  • Worked disease examples (direction stated): IL17RA transmembrane-domain sQTL mediated 90.41% of the sSNP effect on plasma protein (P = 1.14 × 10⁻⁴³), no missense tag SNP; IL6R sQTL mediated only 4.67% (lead in r² > 0.99 with the rs2228145 ADAM-cleavage missense variant) — soluble isoform via both splicing and proteolysis. rs6897932(T): less IL7R exon-6 excision → lower plasma IL-7R → reduced dermatitis/eczema risk (coloc H4 100%/100%/94%). rs724391(C): less WARS1 exon-10 excision → higher plasma WARS1 → increased hypertension risk (H4 100%/100%/95%). OAS1: eQTL + seven sQTLs colocalize with COVID-19 (adjusting for OAS1 expression did not ablate sQTL, P < 1 × 10⁻¹⁶). IFNAR2: eQTL + 10 sQTLs (incl. exon 8/9 transmembrane excision) colocalize with COVID-19; eQTLs also trans-sQTLs for 5 IFI27 splicing events.
  • mQTLs colocalized with eQTL/sQTL very often (96%) but were mediated less than pQTLs (6.8% vs 32.6%), attributed to metabolite correlation structure.
  • Methods stack: STAR 2-pass alignment (GRCh38, Ensembl v99), featureCounts, Regtools junction extraction, LeafCutter clustering, tensorQTL for QTL calling, 35 PEER factors (eQTL) / 10 splicing PCs (sQTL), genotypes imputed with 1000G Phase 3 + UK10K.
  • Caveats stated by authors: single annotation-free splicing definition (other splicing aspects missed); limited trans power (prioritized cis-eSNPs); plasma (not intracellular) proteins, so effects conflate quantity and solubility; metabolite correlation structure; cohort is European ancestry only.

Connections

  • A multi-omic, disease-oriented sibling to zhang-2020-regional-variation-of-splicing-qtls (brain-region sQTL atlas) and park-2018-the-expanding-landscape-of-alternative — extends the splicing-qtl line from tissue/region cataloguing toward variant-to-protein-to-disease mechanism via colocalization + mediation.
  • Splicing quantification rests on leafcutter (li-2018-annotation-free-quantification-of-rna), the same annotation-free intron-ratio approach; contrast with PSI/event-based tools like rmats used in zhang-2020.
  • Mechanistic core (transmembrane-domain excision → soluble isoforms; QKI as a trans splice-factor) ties to splicing-factors and rna-binding-proteins; the disease links sit in splicing-and-disease.
  • Complements protein/binding resources such as van-nostrand-2020-a-large-scale-binding-and-functional by supplying the population-genetics layer (which variants change splicing in vivo and what they do to circulating proteins and disease).
  • Primary deliverable is the intervalrna-portal resource.

Open questions

  • Would single-cell / cell-type-resolved population QTL mapping (which the authors flag as the next step) reassign bulk-blood sQTL signals to specific immune cell types and sharpen mediation estimates?
  • How transferable are these European-ancestry sQTL→protein→disease links to other ancestries?
  • For loci where sQTLs but not eQTLs colocalize with pQTLs (e.g. IL-6R, IL-7R), how generally does transmembrane-domain splicing govern the soluble vs membrane-bound protein balance across the secretome?
  • Can large meta-analyzed trans-QTL resources (e.g. eQTLGen phase II) confirm the QKI-class trans-splicing hubs that bulk RNA-seq power could only partially resolve?
