Cross-ancestry atlas of gene, isoform, and splicing regulation in the developing human brain

One-line: A cross-ancestry xQTL atlas of 672 developing human neocortex samples (4–39 postconception weeks) that uniformly maps gene, isoform, and local-splicing QTLs (splicing-qtl), finds isoform/splicing regulation mediates more neuropsychiatric GWAS heritability than gene expression, and is published as the DevBrainHub resource (devbrainhub).

Key takeaways

  • Most neuropsychiatric GWAS variants are noncoding and enriched in regulatory elements active during brain development, but no unified, population-scale, ancestrally diverse fetal-brain regulatory atlas existed. This study fills that gap by integrating 5 cohorts into 672 distinct developing-neocortex donors and uniformly processing genotype + RNA-seq.
  • Three molecular readouts were mapped as cis-QTLs (collectively “xQTLs”): gene expression (eQTL), Salmon-imputed isoform expression (isoQTL), and leafcutter-quantified annotation-free local splicing / intron-excision ratios (sQTL). 15,752 genes harbored at least one cis-xQTL.
  • The reference is highly diverse — European (~45%), Latino/admixed American (~25%), African-American (~22%), East/Southeast Asian (~8%) — and cross-ancestry data improved statistical fine-mapping resolution (smaller credible sets), not merely via larger N but via distinct LD structure across ancestries.
  • Genetic regulation is developmentally dynamic: cis-heritability of gene expression and local splicing drops sharply from ~10 to ~18 PCW, coinciding with a shift from neural progenitors to maturing excitatory neurons (cellular heterogeneity) plus intrinsic neuronal-maturation effects.
  • Isoform-level regulation mediated the largest share of GWAS heritability across five psychiatric disorders, more than gene expression or local splicing — underscoring the value of isoform/splicing-qtl readouts for interpreting disease variants (splicing-and-disease).
  • Sibling to other brain sQTL resources here such as zhang-2020-regional-variation-of-splicing-qtls and the microglia isoform atlas humphrey-2025-long-read-rna-sequencing-atlas; results are browsable via the devbrainhub portal.

Notable claims / data

  • 672 distinct donors integrated; after strict QC, 654 samples with matched genotype + cortical RNA-seq spanning 4–39 PCW (Tri1 N=216, Tri2 N=433); imputed into the multiancestry TOPMed panel.
  • QTL counts (FDR < 0.05): 10,094 eGenes, 11,845 isoGenes (cis-isoQTL), 7,490 sGenes (cis-sQTL). Of 15,752 xQTL genes, 3,740 shared across all three; 2,312 isoform-only and 1,891 local-splicing-only — i.e. isoQTLs/sQTLs capture orthogonal signal beyond eQTLs (Storey’s pi1).
  • 2,488 “fetal-specific” eGenes vs adult PsychENCODE (N=1,387) and GTEx (N=205); these are more loss-of-function intolerant (higher pLI) and enriched for spliceosomal pathways and ventricular radial glia markers. isoGenes and sGenes are also more LoF-intolerant than eGenes.
  • Effect sizes (allelic fold change) highly concordant developing-vs-adult (GTEx rho=0.93; PsychENCODE rho=0.89) and across ancestries (AMR–EUR and AFR–EUR rho=0.97). AFR, despite smallest N (135), gave the smallest credible sets; example gene MTFR1 CS shrank from EUR=49/AMR=57 to AFR=9, multiancestry=5, trans-ancestry+functional=3.
  • 49 inversion-associated eQTLs (cis and trans) at four large recurrent inversions (17q21.31, 16p11.2, 7q11.22, 8p23.1); longer inversions more likely to affect expression.
  • Trimester split: ~2-fold more Tri1 than Tri2 eGenes (4,211 vs 2,220) and sGenes (5,312 vs 2,318); cis-h2SNP drops Tri1→Tri2 and developing→adult; linear decline 10–18 weeks confirmed by sliding-window analysis (N=150 batches). phNPC vs differentiated-neuron comparison supports an intrinsic-maturation contribution beyond cell-type proportion shifts.
  • MESC mediation across SCZ, ASD, BIP, ADHD, MDD: isoform regulation mediated 6.45 ± 1.92% of h2, vs gene expression 3.15 ± 1.25% and splicing 2.13 ± 1.74% — isoform > gene > splicing; Tri2 isoform regulation especially important (notably BIP). Overall mediation small but consistent, robust to excluding high-pLI and to protein-coding-only restriction.
  • Colocalization (eCAVIAR CLPP>0.01 / COLOC PP4>0.7) prioritized 293 of 485 psychiatric GWAS loci (~60%), with >2-fold more colocalizations than larger adult-brain panels (57 vs 20 eQTL colocalizations for SCZ+BIP). More loci colocalized via isoQTLs/sQTLs than eQTLs.
  • Convergence of common and rare variant signal at SCZ risk gene SP4: risk allele rs10276352 (G>A) is an sQTL increasing inclusion of a 181-bp cryptic, unannotated exon (chr7:21521120–21521300), introducing a frameshift/premature stop predicted to trigger nonsense-mediated decay and remove the DNA-binding zinc-finger domain — a mechanism invisible to eQTL mapping (no SP4 eQTL in fetal or adult brain). Other examples: FURIN isoQTL (SCZ/BIP), SCN2A splicing (BIP), GRM3 downregulation (SCZ), KMT2E isoform dysregulation (SCZ).
  • isoTWAS: 536 isoforms across 271 genes significant; fine-mapping gave 129 putatively causal isoforms (107 genes); 14 isoforms prioritized by both isoTWAS and isoQTL colocalization (e.g. SLC9C2, KMT2E, ABCB9). MRLocus added causal support for 7 of 14 multi-eQTL TWAS genes.
  • Networks: rWGCNA yielded 124 gene/isoform coexpression modules (isoform-level for >120,000 transcripts); isoform modules better captured cell-type marker enrichment (OR=2.37). 8,008 module-interaction QTLs (ieQTLs); 3,960 module-specific; ieQTLs added 22 SCZ colocalizations (13 not seen with bulk eQTLs, e.g. BRINP2 × module M93). CellWalker mapped 3,739 (21.7%) of eQTL genes to a specific cellular context. GWAS/rare-variant risk localized to maturing excitatory and interneuron modules, depleted in neural-progenitor modules.

Connections

  • A flagship human developing-brain splicing-qtl / isoQTL resource, squarely in splicing-and-disease; the SP4 cryptic-exon NMD finding is a concrete fetal-brain allele-specific-splicing-type mechanism linking a risk allele to altered intron-cluster usage.
  • Splicing quantified with annotation-free leafcutter intron-excision ratios; isoform expression imputed with Salmon (cf. li-2018-annotation-free-quantification-of-rna, bray-2016-near-optimal-probabilistic-rna-seq-quantification in the wiki’s quantification lineage).
  • data-quality-and-bias: uniform reprocessing across 5 heterogeneous cohorts was essential — sQTLs replicated within Walker (pi1=0.93) and HDBR (pi1=0.89) after uniform remapping, showing robustness to library-prep/sequencing-depth batch effects; heritability checks controlled for hidden covariates / cell-type composition.
  • Sibling brain/sQTL resources: zhang-2020-regional-variation-of-splicing-qtls (adult brain regional sQTLs) and humphrey-2025-long-read-rna-sequencing-atlas (microglia isoform/sQTL atlas), both of which likewise find splicing/isoform QTLs colocalizing with neuropsychiatric/neurodegenerative risk beyond eQTLs.
  • Complements developing-brain proteome work wu-2026-single-cell-proteomic-landscape-of and the disease-mechanism review scotti-2015-rna-mis-splicing-in-disease.

Open questions

  • Why does cis-heritability fall so steeply across 10–18 PCW — how much is cellular heterogeneity vs intrinsic neuronal-maturation chromatin/regulatory change, and is the same true for splicing specifically?
  • Can the SP4 cryptic-exon / NMD mechanism (and similar sQTL-driven poison-exon events) be experimentally validated and therapeutically targeted?
  • Single-cell or long-read resolution of isoform usage in fetal cortex would test whether bulk isoQTLs/sQTLs reflect specific cell types vs mixtures (the ieQTL/CellWalker approach is an approximation).
  • How transferable are these cross-ancestry fine-mapped causal variants to under-sampled populations (EA/SEA were only ~8%)?
