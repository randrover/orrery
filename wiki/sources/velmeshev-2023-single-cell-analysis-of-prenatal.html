Single-cell analysis of prenatal and postnatal human cortical development

One-line: A single-nucleus RNA + chromatin atlas of >700,000 nuclei spanning second-trimester gestation through adulthood that charts lineage-specific developmental gene programs of human cortical cell types and pinpoints the cell types, developmental stages, and (notably female-biased) gene programs most enriched for autism genetic risk. Data are browsable via the human-cortical-development-cell-browser.

Key takeaways

  • Builds a comprehensive transcriptomic atlas of human cortical development across prenatal and postnatal life, deliberately covering the third trimester, birth, and early postnatal stages that prior single-cell work (largely confined to the second trimester) under-sampled — a functional-single-cell-genomics resource.
  • Single-cell trajectory analysis reconstructs developmental programs for subtypes of excitatory neurons, interneurons, glia (astrocytes, OPCs, oligodendrocytes, microglia), and brain vasculature, with pseudotime tracking real developmental age.
  • Integrating snRNA-seq with single-nucleus open-chromatin (snATAC-seq) data defines enhancer gene regulatory networks (eGRNs) and candidate transcription factors driving lineage commitment.
  • Intersecting lineage- and sex-specific programs with disease risk-gene sets localizes genetic vulnerability: ASD/SCZ/BPD risk genes are enriched in second-trimester programs and in deep-layer excitatory neurons; AD risk genes are enriched in microglia.
  • A central finding: lineage-specific genes up-regulated in female cells are especially enriched for autism risk genes, supporting an ASD “female protective effect” and offering a mechanistic account of the male bias in autism.
  • Methodologically and biologically a close sibling of the Kriegstein-lab proteomic study wu-2026-single-cell-proteomic-landscape-of (developing human cortex, single-cell resolution, autism-vulnerability focus); the two are complementary RNA/chromatin vs protein views of the same developmental system.

Notable claims / data

  • Generated snRNA-seq from 413,682 nuclei / 108 tissue samples / 60 neurotypical individuals; after debris removal 358,663 nuclei retained; after integrating published datasets the final atlas is 709,372 nuclei, 169 tissue samples, 106 donors (45 female, 61 male). Median 1,106 genes / 1,609 transcripts per nucleus.
  • Sampled prefrontal, cingulate, temporal, insular, and motor cortices plus the ganglionic eminences (interneuron source). 10x Genomics 3′ v2 capture; reads aligned to unspliced transcriptome to capture pre-mRNA; integration via Harmony, clustering/UMAP via Seurat, trajectories via Monocle 3 (cross-checked with Slingshot for microglia).
  • Identified 1,062 lineage-specific and 405 branch-specific genes. Neuronal lineages split into two maturation tempos: those maturing by end of second trimester (L5, L5-6-IT, all interneurons) vs protracted maturation through third trimester and after birth (L2-3, L4, L6) — evidence of a protracted human neuronal maturation timeline.
  • Expression dynamics dominated by transient vs burst patterns: broad-branch genes tend transient, mature-cell-type genes tend burst — suggesting gradual commitment via transient+burst transcriptional events. Putative regulators include MN1 (L2-3/L5-6-IT/L4), HS3ST4 (L5), noncoding CYP1B1-AS1 / LINC00507 (L2-3).
  • snATAC-seq: combined 284,907 profiles / 57 samples / 42 individuals from four datasets; SCENIC+ identified 51 TFs regulating 1,373 lineage-specific genes via 4,846 predicted regulatory regions. Recovered known regulators (SOX5, LHX6, OLIG2, SOX9) and newly implicated ones (BACH2 in L5; NFIX, ZNF184 → CUX2 in L2-3; MAFB in PV interneurons; FOXN2, RFX4 in glia).
  • Sex programs: 740 female-enriched vs 312 male-enriched dynamically expressed genes. Female genes → CNS development, cell adhesion, synaptic transmission; male genes → RNA metabolism / translation. >90% of sex-enriched genes peak in the second trimester (early, transient sex modulation). Sex enrichment not driven by sex-chromosome transcription (despite XIST, PCDH11Y among top hits).
  • Disease enrichment: ASD risk (SFARI score 2/3, and high-confidence ASD-HC by TADA) enriched in L5-6-IT and L5 neurons; female-specific developmental genes strongly enriched in both SFARI and ASD-HC lists, with subplate and L6 neurons prominent (e.g. NR4A2, MEF2C, NRXN2, PCDH15 up in female cells). Authors invoke this to explain the ~4:1 male:female ASD ratio via greater male susceptibility to haploinsufficiency.
  • Synaptic-pruning complement genes up-regulated in microglia, and glutamate-transport maturation in protoplasmic astrocytes, both peaking between birth and ~1 year — flagged as a critical postnatal synaptic-plasticity window.
  • Caveats stated: difficulty integrating snRNA-seq with snATAC-seq; microglia/vascular lineages omitted from chromatin analysis due to low ATAC profile counts; first-trimester and some intermediate stages not included; 3D/epigenetic imprinting questions left open.

Connections

  • A developing-human-brain single-cell resource that pairs directly with wu-2026-single-cell-proteomic-landscape-of (same lab/system, proteomic layer) — both converge on autism vulnerability in the prenatal cortex from different molecular modalities.
  • The integrative snRNA + snATAC + eGRN design is a functional-single-cell-genomics multiomic approach conceptually adjacent to other single-cell regulatory-mapping efforts in the wiki such as chi-2026-single-cell-mapping-of-regulatory.
  • Disease-risk enrichment via SFARI/GWAS gene sets parallels developing-brain functional-genomics efforts like li-2018-integrative-functional-genomic-analysis and parikshak-2016-genome-wide-changes-in-lncrna.
  • Interactive atlas exposed through the human-cortical-development-cell-browser.

Open questions

  • Can earlier (first-trimester) and missing intermediate stages be integrated to complete the developmental trajectory, given scRNA-seq/snRNA-seq integration hurdles?
  • Are upper-layer (L2-3) neuron disturbances specific to idiopathic ASD, and driven by common vs rare variants?
  • Does genomic imprinting contribute to the observed sex enrichment of developmentally expressed genes (single-cell epigenetic profiling needed)?
  • Cell-type- and stage-specific vulnerability for BPD and SCZ remains preliminary and needs deeper genetic dissection.
  • Mechanistic test of whether higher baseline female expression of ASD risk genes confers resistance to haploinsufficiency.
