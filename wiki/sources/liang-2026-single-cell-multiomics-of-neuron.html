Single-cell multiomics of neuron activation reveals context-specific genetics of brain disorders

One-line: Single-nucleus RNA+ATAC multiomics of iPSC-derived excitatory and inhibitory neurons from 100 donors, stimulated with KCl (0/1/6 h) to model neuronal activation, showing that most genetic effects relevant to neuropsychiatric disorders (NPD) are context-specific — eQTLs, caQTLs, allele-specific open chromatin (ASoC), and cTWAS-prioritized risk genes appear or strengthen only upon activation, with caQTLs explaining a far larger share of NPD heritability than eQTLs and activation implicating cholesterol/lipid metabolism in disease.

Key takeaways

  • Models activity-dependent gene regulation at population scale: glutamatergic (iGlut) + GABAergic (iGABA) neurons differentiated from 100 human iPSC lines, cocultured with rat astrocytes, depolarized with 53 mM KCl for 1 or 6 h, then assayed by single-nucleus multiomics (snRNA-seq + snATAC-seq) at 0/1/6 h — the resting post-mortem brain misses biology that only switches on with activity.
  • 1,053,422 nuclei (651,012 passed QC) resolve three neural subtypes — GABA, NEFM⁺ Glut (npglut), NEFM⁻ Glut (nmglut) — each showing widespread activation-induced transcriptomic + epigenomic change: early response genes (ERGs, peak ~1 h, e.g. FOS, NPAS4) then late response genes (LRGs, peak ~6 h, e.g. BDNF, VGF).
  • Gene expression and chromatin accessibility are often discordant — “epigenetic memory” (open chromatin persists after expression drops) and “chromatin priming” (accessibility opens before expression rises), complicating the activation regulatory code (chromatin-accessibility).
  • ERG clusters are enriched for cholesterol-biosynthesis genes and NPD GWAS signal; LRG clusters for axon guidance / synaptic processes — surfacing lipid metabolism as an activation-regulated axis of NPD.
  • Activation-dependent gene regulatory networks (GRN inference) highlight ASD-risk transcription factors MEF2C, TCF4, RORB, MLXIP; CRISPR knockout of RORB, MEF2C, TCF4 validated predicted ASD-target dysregulation (autism).
  • Mapped stimulation-specific molecular QTLs: eQTLs, chromatin-accessibility QTLs (caQTLs), and ASoC variants — many detectable only after activation. caQTLs explained ~25% of SCZ heritability vs ~10% for eQTLs, and stimulation-specific QTLs carried stronger GWAS enrichment.
  • Causal-TWAS (ctwas) integrating these QTLs with GWAS prioritized putatively causal NPD risk genes predominantly under stimulated conditions (e.g. CPT1C for SCZ), and SCZ patient–derived neurons up-regulated cholesterol synthesis upon activation.

Notable claims / data

  • Cohort/assay: 100 iPSC lines → Day-35 iGlut (NEUROG2) + iGABA (ASCL1+DLX2) neurons + rat astrocytes; 53 mM KCl, 0/1/6 h; 10x Genomics Single-Cell Multiome (GEX+ATAC). Subtype counts: GABA n=251,501; npglut n=159,735; nmglut n=137,564. iGlut/iGABA most resemble second-trimester–to–2-year human neurons.
  • OCR–gene links: 8,522 OCR–gene pairs (4,017 genes) in GABA, 6,921 (3,625) nmglut, 7,492 (3,651) npglut; 43–49% of “coactivation” OCR–gene pairs overlap activity-by-contact (ABC) enhancer–gene pairs (>2.5-fold enrichment). ~150,000–300,000 OCR peaks across types/times, 10–22% stimulation-specific; only DA peaks at 1/6 h (not static peaks) showed NPD GWAS enrichment.
  • GRN/TF: 145 candidate early-response TF regulators (half shared across all cell types); only 6 of 64 late-response TFs shared across cell types (late response is more cell-type-specific, as with LRGs). GABA-specific TCF4 (SCZ master regulator) elevated even before stimulation.
  • eQTL: 1,316–4,113 eGenes per context (cell type × time); 8–11% of eGenes only in stimulated states; eGene numbers larger from stimulated conditions; 19–61% of eGenes shared with GTEx brain; effect sizes correlate r ≈ 0.71–0.76 with GTEx cerebellum. Dynamic eGenes: 288–976 across contexts; dynamic eQTLs more cell-type-specific and less shared with GTEx than static — i.e. activation reveals QTLs missed by bulk brain.
  • caQTL/ASoC: 1,800–11,600 cPeaks (≥1 caQTL) per context, >2× more upon stimulation; 5,461–9,832 caQTLs dynamic. 4,900–18,700 ASoC variants across nine contexts; caQTL vs ASoC effect sizes r=0.82; ASoC enriched 30–80-fold in eQTLs of the matching context. Stimulation-specific ASoC enriched in enhancers and AP-1 (FOS/JUNB) motifs at 1/6 h vs ASCL1/DLX1/2, CUX2 at 0 h.
  • Heritability: caQTLs explained substantially larger NPD h²g than eQTLs (≈25% vs ≈10% for SCZ cPeaks), even controlling for eQTL effects — chromatin variants capture regulatory effects expression QTLs miss.
  • cTWAS: SCZ had most candidate genes (48 at PIP>0.5; 19 high-confidence PIP>0.8). Most SCZ/NPD cTWAS genes are “dynamic” (PIP higher in stimulated states). Only a few overlap GTEx-brain-derived TWAS genes (e.g. FOXN2, NPIPB2, TDRD6, ZNF823) — stimulation-driven eQTLs discover risk genes static brain QTLs miss. MADL1L SCZ cPeak driven by a single caQTL.
  • Cholesterol/SCZ: CPT1C (carnitine palmitoyltransferase 1C) is a SCZ cTWAS gene whose eQTL effect appears chiefly at 1 h stimulation in nmglut; it maintains neuronal lipid metabolism for synaptic activity. SCZ C6 ERG module (cholesterol-biosynthesis-enriched, NPD-GWAS-enriched): 5 of 6 cholesterol genes (e.g. INSIG1, HMGCS1, MSMO1, HMGCR, CYP51A1) differentially expressed in SCZ neurons only upon activation; SREBF1/SREBF2 TFs (lipid/cholesterol regulators) have targets enriched for ASD genes.
  • Caveats (authors): iPSC QTL mapping may be underpowered; KCl is a strong, possibly non-physiological proxy for in vivo neural activation; only two stimulation time points; some activity-dependent genes (e.g. FOS) show diurnal rhythmicity that could confound; statistical prioritization ≠ definitive causal inference — functional validation still required.

Connections

  • Activation as the missing context. This is the wiki’s first population-scale activity-dependent genetics dataset — it operationalizes neuronal-activation and supplies the dynamic / context-specific QTL evidence that static post-mortem brain QTL atlases (e.g. bryois-2022-cell-type-specific-cis-eqtls-in-eight, li-2018-integrative-functional-genomic-analysis, zhang-2020-regional-variation-of-splicing-qtls) structurally cannot see.
  • Chromatin > expression for heritability. caQTLs/ASoC explaining ~2.5× more SCZ h²g than eQTLs extends chromatin-accessibility as the prime substrate for noncoding-variant interpretation, and speaks to the missing-heritability debate (context-specific regulatory variance is part of the “missing” share).
  • Causal-gene prioritization. ctwas here is the disease-mapping engine, a cousin of TWAS/isotwas; it complements direct functional assays (massively-parallel-reporter-assay, pooled-crispr-screen) — and the paper’s own CRISPR-KO validation of RORB/MEF2C/TCF4 closes that loop for autism.
  • Cholesterol thesis. cholesterol-and-brain-disorders connects an unexpected metabolic axis (lipid/cholesterol synthesis, CPT1C) to SCZ/ASD, joining the synaptic/developmental story that dominates psychiatric-genetics.
  • A resource-tagged iPSC-neuron activation atlas, sibling to the chromatin-accessibility / single-cell atlases the artificial-intelligence topic’s models learn from.

Open questions

  • Does KCl depolarization faithfully recapitulate physiological/in-vivo neural activity, and would patterned or receptor-driven stimulation (and more time points) reveal additional dynamic QTLs?
  • Is cholesterol/lipid dysregulation a cause or consequence of NPD-relevant neuronal activation — and could cholesterol-targeting drugs (the CPT1C/SREBF axis) act on clinical symptoms?
  • How much further NPD “missing heritability” is hidden in other unmodeled contexts (developmental stage, other cell types, other stimuli)?
  • Will functional validation confirm the cTWAS-prioritized causal genes (e.g. CPT1C, MADL1L, ZNF823) beyond statistical prioritization?
