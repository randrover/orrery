Uncovering spatially resolved functional genomics with CRISPR screen sequencing

One-line: Introduces spac-seq, a sequencing-based spatial CRISPR screen platform that captures pooled sgRNA libraries alongside single-cell-resolution whole transcriptomes in situ, plus tardis-tool, a statistical toolkit for spatial perturbation analysis — together enabling transcriptome-wide functional genomics with spatial phenotypes in vivo.

Key takeaways

  • spac-seq (spatial CRISPR screen sequencing) couples a pooled sgRNA library (delivered via a retroviral SPACseq plasmid) with single-cell-resolution spatial transcriptomics platforms (10x Visium HD, Stereo-seq), capturing both sgRNA and whole transcriptome in situ via NGS — addressing the gap that prior spatial CRISPR screens were imaging-based and not transcriptome-wide.
  • The plasmid embeds the sgRNA within the GFP mRNA (transcribed by RNA Pol II) so it is captured like any transcript, plus a U6/Pol III cassette; this avoids a separate barcode library and its recombination risk. It also supports direct probe capture of the sgRNA spacer+scaffold (Visium HD).
  • tardis-tool (target prioritization toolkit for perturbation data in spatial omics) handles sgRNA-transcriptome spatial alignment, library preprocessing, statistical modeling of global effects and local perturbation enrichments, and spatial pathway analysis.
  • Applied in mouse syngeneic tumor models to three biology questions: (1) Icam1 loss in tumor cells promotes lung-metastasis colonization and immune exclusion; (2) Cd44 in CD8+ T cells regulates spatial localization via Spp1 on macrophages; (3) a transcription factor-chemokine receptor axis couples cell state to chemotaxis.
  • A pooled perturbation/spatial-omics method conceptually adjacent to single-cell-perturbation-modeling but readout-based and in vivo rather than predictive; relevant to ai-in-genomics for the statistical/spatial-niche modeling in TARDIS.

Notable claims / data

  • sgRNA detection accuracy: mRNA-embedded vs genomic DNA sequencing near-perfect in a CD8+ T cell mini-pool (Pearson ρ = 0.9970), beating CROP-seq (ρ = 0.8913); held at scale with a 9,040-sgRNA library (ρ = 0.9414) and direct capture (ρ = 0.9596).
  • SPACseq plasmid showed 9.08-fold higher mRNA-embedded sgRNA expression than CROP-seq, and higher transduction efficiency in MC38 and primary CD8+ T cells; Cd8a KO efficiency 75.19% (vs CROP-seq 85.2%, attributed to CROP-seq’s dual-sgRNA cassette).
  • In vivo 1,520-sgRNA MC38 subQ screen: recovered 1,393 (91.64%) sgRNAs by direct capture and 1,161 (76.38%) by mRNA embedding per section; 1,068/1,520 (70.26%) shared across both methods and mice. Top depleted hits enriched for MHC presentation (H2-T23, B2m, H2-K1, Ifngr2, H2-Q7); validated B4galt1.
  • Single-cell resolution: StarDist segmentation assigned 95.54% of sgRNA counts to single cells; 44.58% of cells had a single unambiguous sgRNA (1.51 sgRNA UMIs/cell). Serial-section reproducibility: pairwise Spearman > 0.92; 85.87% of perturbations spatially consistent (FDR < 0.05), median SSIM 0.9809 at perturbation level.
  • Metastasis screen: lung metastases harvested 14 days post-IV injection; TARDIS identified 138 and 48 of 221 clonal perturbations enriched in immune-infiltrated vs immune-excluded niches. sgIcam1 was both highly expanded and immune-excluded; sgIcam1 region was T cell-poor / macrophage-rich, with M2 polarization and elevated Spp1/Cd163 vs Cxcl9/Cxcl10. mIF validation: significantly larger tumor area (p<0.001) and reduced CD8+ T cell infiltration on Icam1 loss.
  • T cell screen: two-step design — bulk in vivo infiltration screen (2,135 genes / 9,040 sgRNAs, 99.62% recovered, MAGeCK; positive controls Zc3h12a/Regnase-1, Gata3, Klrd1, Ddit3, Nmb, Piezo1) then a spatiotemporal mini-pool (top 33 hits, tumors at days 4/7/10), identifying Cd44 as a regulator of T cell spatial localization interacting with macrophage Spp1.
  • Caveats: demonstrated in mouse syngeneic tumor models; sgRNA-transcript capture is subject to spatial-transcriptomics dropout (only ~0.8–1 UMI per 8–10 μm bin); spatial sgRNA vs tissue-NGS correlations are modest (ρ ≈ 0.28–0.47).

Connections

  • functional-single-cell-genomics — extends pooled CRISPR screens / Perturb-seq / CROP-seq from dissociated single cells to spatially resolved in vivo phenotypes; benchmarked directly against the CROP-seq sgRNA-embedding system.
  • single-cell-perturbation-modeling — a complementary, experimental (rather than predictive/generative) route to linking perturbations to cell-state phenotypes; sibling in spirit to in-silico methods like cmonge and txpert but measures spatial phenotypes directly.
  • ai-in-genomics — TARDIS’s statistical niche/program modeling and StarDist segmentation are the computational backbone enabling target prioritization from spatial readouts.

Open questions

  • Can SPAC-seq be ported from mouse syngeneic models to human tissue / patient-derived systems despite spatial-transcriptomics dropout?
  • How well do the modest spatial-vs-bulk sgRNA correlations (ρ ≈ 0.28–0.47) constrain quantitative effect-size estimates per niche?
  • Could predictive perturbation models (e.g. single-cell-perturbation-modeling) be trained on SPAC-seq spatial readouts to forecast niche-level effects?
