A neuron type-specific microexon in Ank3/ankyrin-G modulates calcium activity and neuronal excitability

One-line: Functional dissection of a deeply conserved 27-nt microexon (E35a) in Ank3/ankyrin-G that is included in GABAergic but skipped in glutamatergic cortical neurons, showing that this single alternative-splicing event tunes intracellular Ca²⁺ signaling and interneuron excitability independently of ankyrin-G’s canonical axon-initial-segment role.

Key takeaways

  • Provides a rare example of connecting a neuron-type-specific alternative exon to actual cellular and organismal phenotype (splicing-and-neurodevelopment) — most such exons are catalogued but not functionally validated.
  • E35a is a 27-nt microexons in Ank3 (ankyrin-G / AnkG), predominantly included in cortical GABAergic interneurons (PSI ~0.65) and cerebellar neurons but skipped in glutamatergic excitatory neurons (PSI ~0.14); inclusion is neuron-type-specific and skipped in non-neuronal cells (neuronal-cell-types).
  • Splicing of E35a is under combinatorial control by multiple splicing-factors / rna-binding-proteins: Ptbp2 represses it in non-neuronal/young cells; Celf2 and nSR100/SRRM4 promote inclusion; Mbnl2 (higher in glutamatergic neurons) is the major repressor driving glutamatergic skipping; Elavl2 and Qk are enriched in GABAergic neurons. Inclusion is also neuronal-activity-dependent (reduced after KCl depolarization).
  • E35a and its flanking introns are conserved across >450 million years of vertebrate evolution (mouse to fish), implying strong purifying selection and functional significance; a paralogous exon exists in Ank2 but not Ank1.
  • Ank3 E35a⁻ᐟ⁻ mice: GABAergic interneurons (especially fast-spiking) show increased intrinsic excitability (higher input resistance, lower rheobase, higher firing) and elevated somatic Ca²⁺ activity — without disruption of the axon initial segment (AIS length/position and action-potential initiation preserved).
  • Mechanistically, the E35a-encoded 9-amino-acid peptide (in an intrinsically disordered region between the UPA and death domains of AnkG) facilitates AnkG binding to the NCX/NKA/InsP3R (IP3R1) complex at ER–plasma-membrane junctions, important for intracellular Ca²⁺ handling — a role parallel to AnkB’s in cardiac cells.

Notable claims / data

  • Systematic scRNA-seq analysis of adult mouse neocortex found 469 cassette exons differentially spliced between glutamatergic and GABAergic neurons; E35a (27 nt, between constitutive exons E35 and E36, upstream of the giant exon E37) is one of them.
  • The homologous AnkB peptide differs: the Ank2 exon has six extra nucleotides (encodes 11 aa) and is constitutively included; an E1425G missense mutation in the AnkB homologous region causes human type-4 long-QT syndrome by abrogating AnkB–InsP3R interaction — motivating the Ca²⁺ hypothesis.
  • Minigene reporter assays: nSR100 overexpression raised E35a inclusion to ~20%; co-expressed Mbnl2 dose-dependently repressed inclusion to ~12%, validating combinatorial regulation (confirmed in N2a and HEK293T cells). E35a has a weak 3′ splice site (3′SS score −0.98 vs 5′SS 7.96 in the human homolog), consistent with the upstream intron being the main regulatory region.
  • Two-photon in vivo Ca²⁺ imaging (GCaMP6s) in prelimbic mPFC: E35a⁻ᐟ⁻ interneurons showed ~2-fold higher Ca²⁺ activity (AUC 8.05 vs 4.75; higher amplitude, duration, frequency), while glutamatergic pyramidal neurons were largely normal — matching the neuron-type-specific inclusion pattern.
  • Co-IP from cerebellum: E35a deletion reduced AnkG–InsP3R1 interaction by 77% and AnkG–NKA (Na-K ATPase) interaction by 66%; a FLAG UPA-DD minigene lacking E35a pulled down ~30% less InsP3R1 than the E35a-containing construct, showing the microexon peptide directly mediates the protein-protein interaction.
  • E35a⁻ᐟ⁻ mice were born at Mendelian ratios with grossly normal development; behavioral phenotypes were mild (increased step length and locomotion/gait, hyperactive tendency) despite Ank3’s link to bipolar disorder and schizophrenia — suggesting compensatory adjustments in excitatory/inhibitory balance.

Connections

  • A functional-genomics case study extending the neuron-type-specific microexon theme from nikom-2023-alternative-splicing-in-neurodegenerative-disease and baralle-2017-alternative-splicing-as-a-regulator (SRRM4/nSR100-regulated microexons, misregulated in autism); grounds splicing-and-neurodevelopment and neuronal-cell-types.
  • Deep on combinatorial splicing-factors / rna-binding-proteins logic (Ptbp2, Celf2, nSR100/SRRM4, Mbnl2, Elavl2, Qk) overlapping regulators discussed across the wiki’s splicing pages; connects to splicing-code and percent-spliced-in (PSI quantification).
  • Activity-dependent inclusion links to neuronal-activation biology; the AnkG/AIS angle and bipolar/SCZ/ASD genetic associations connect to autism and psychiatric-genetics; the Ca²⁺/InsP3R mechanism introduces a new microexons concept page.
  • Uses CRISPR-Cas9 to delete a specific microexon in vivo — a splicing-perturbation method complementing the splice-switching/ASO approaches in desterro-2020-targeting-mrna-processing-as-an and nikom-2023-alternative-splicing-in-neurodegenerative-disease.

Open questions

  • Does the ER-storage / ER-PM-junction Ca²⁺ model hold up to direct observation of the NCX/NKA/InsP3R complex at junctions and its dependence on E35a inclusion in vivo?
  • Why are the behavioral phenotypes so mild given robust cellular changes — what compensatory circuits rebalance excitation/inhibition, and would inbred-background or cognitive tasks reveal deficits?
  • How generally do neuron-type-specific microexons in scaffolding/adaptor proteins reprogram protein-interaction networks to diversify neuronal physiology?
  • Is there a parallel AnkB/cardiac vs AnkG/neuronal splicing mechanism representing reused machinery, and does human ANK3 E35a mis-splicing contribute to bipolar disorder?
